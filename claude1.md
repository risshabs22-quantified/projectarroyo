1) Home Page:

This project uses QGIS mapping, NASA's ARSET data information, and field observations to study a local urban natural corridor and identify areas where native plant restoration may be most needed. It combines digital mapping, tree canopy/shade analysis, site photos, habitat notes, and a scoring system to compare restoration potential across different public green spaces.

2) Problem:

Many urban natural areas face environmental stress from uneven shade, invasive plants, habitat disturbance, trash, erosion, heavy public use, and nearby development. These issues can weaken native plant communities and reduce habitat quality for wildlife and pollinators. This project aims to identify which areas have the greatest restoration need and potential so future planting or conservation efforts can be more targeted and effective.

9) About:

Project Arroyo is an independent student research project focused on using QGIS mapping, field observations, and NASA ARSET remote sensing concepts to study native plant restoration opportunities in natural green spaces.
I started this project because I believe environmental science can connect many different disciplines, including conservation, habitat restoration, remote sensing, and geospatial mapping. Through this project, I am learning how tools such as species distribution modeling, Google Earth Engine, and GIS data can help identify real environmental problems and support more evidence-based solutions.
My goal is to create clear, evidence-based restoration priority maps that show where native planting and habitat improvement may have the greatest environmental benefit. Project Arroyo is also a foundational project that helps me explore how mapping, field research, and conservation planning can shape my future work in environmental science.
